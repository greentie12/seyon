Parachute Abstract

Parachutes have many novel applications including NASAâ€™s space travel missions and military operations. Parachutes with enhanced performance and safety are needed to perform these functions. The goal of this project was to design a parachute with improved performance using novel canopy materials. Five different canopy materials were tested: Electrospun Nanofiber-Nylon, Nylon, Rayon, Terylene, and Cotton. Model parachutes of identical size (30 cm diameter) and shape (circle) using these materials were made and dropped from a constant height with an identical weight load. Parachute performance was determined using drag time, drop velocity, canopy performance, descent performance, and path of descent. The Electrospun Nanofiber Nylon canopy parachute performed the best with the longest drag time and slowest drop velocity compared to Nylon and all other materials tested. The Nanofiber Nylon has higher available surface area to interact with the air molecules, which increases air resistance, thus increasing drag time and slowing down drop velocity. This experiment shows that Nanofiber Nylon canopy material enhances parachute performance compared to the current parachutes made with Nylon canopy.
