The Future of Smart Recycling: Can We Improve Responsible Recycling Using Object Recognition?

By: Seyon Wijendran and Amrik Eadara

Sharon High School, Sharon, MA

Massachusetts Science and Engineering Fair 2021 High School State Finals, May 4, 2021 Awarded Second Place

Abstract

Objective: The objective of this project was to develop an object detection neural network model to identify recyclable plastic water bottles with high accuracy of >75%. Background: Plastic pollution has reached crisis proportions in the US. About 91% of recyclable plastic end up in natural environments and plastic water bottles are one of the major contributors to pollution. Technology-integrated automation can improve plastic recycling. Methods: We used TensorFlow with Python API to create the neural network object recognition model to identify common brands of recyclable plastic water bottles with >75% accuracy. We created a customized library with 300 images of the most common brands of plastic water bottles and trained it over 2000 times. We tested the performance of the model by feeding real-time live images of common brands of plastic water bottles and other random objects via the laptopâ€™s webcam and recording the live detection confidence scores (% accuracy), number of trials with accurate labeling, and false-positive results with ten trials. Results: The object recognition neural network model detected and identified the common, popular brands of plastic water bottles with an average live accuracy of >90%. Plastic water bottles were accurately labeled in all trials (10/10) and the pop-up alert message with information on how to recycle plastic water bottles was displayed only when plastic water bottles were detected in all trials (10/10). There was zero (0/10) false positive detection for other objects presented. Conclusion: The customized object recognition model developed and integrated with a webcam was highly accurate and detected recyclable plastic water bottles with > 90% accuracy.
